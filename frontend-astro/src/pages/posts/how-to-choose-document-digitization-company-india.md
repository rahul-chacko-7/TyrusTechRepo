Choosing a document digitization company in India is not just about finding someone with scanners. A good partner should understand paper records, data security, indexing, retrieval and how your team will use the files after conversion.

This checklist can help hospitals, banks, NBFCs, government departments, legal teams, HR teams and enterprise record rooms choose the right vendor.

## 1. Check exact service capability

Ask whether the company provides only scanning or complete document digitization.

A complete service should include:

- Document preparation
- Bulk document scanning
- Onsite document scanning if required
- Image quality checks
- OCR text extraction
- Searchable PDF or PDF/A output
- Metadata capture
- File naming and folder structure
- DMS-ready, EMR-ready, ERP-ready or cloud-ready delivery

For example, Tyrus covers [document scanning services](/document-scanning-services-india), [OCR indexing services](/ocr-indexing-services) and [digital archive services](/services/digital-archives) as connected parts of the same workflow.

## 2. Confirm security and document handling

Many digitization projects involve confidential files such as medical records, KYC forms, loan files, legal contracts, HR folders, invoices and government records.

Ask vendors:

- Who can access the physical files?
- Are staff trained and NDA-backed?
- How are batches tracked?
- How are missing, damaged or unreadable pages reported?
- Is secure transfer available?
- Can scanning happen onsite if documents cannot leave the premises?

For sensitive projects, [onsite document scanning services](/onsite-document-scanning-services) may be the right model.

## 3. Review indexing quality

Scanning creates images. Digitization makes those images useful.

Before work starts, decide what fields users need for search. Examples:

- Patient MRN
- Customer ID
- Account number
- Loan number
- File number
- Vendor name
- Invoice number
- Department
- Year
- Case ID

Good metadata helps users retrieve the right document quickly. Weak indexing can make even a completed scanning project frustrating.

## 4. Compare output formats

Ask what you will receive at the end of the project.

Common output options include:

- Searchable PDF
- PDF/A
- TIFF or JPEG images
- Excel or CSV index sheets
- Structured folders
- DMS import files
- EMR, ERP or cloud-ready archives

If your organization already uses a document management system, the output should match that system instead of forcing your team to reorganize everything later.

## 5. Ask for industry experience

Different records need different workflows.

Healthcare projects need MRD, OPD, IPD and medico-legal retrieval planning. Banking projects need KYC, loan and audit structure. Government projects need citizen records, registers and archive traceability. Legal teams need case-file and contract structure.

Review vendor experience through [case studies](/case-studies) and similar project examples.

## 6. Do not choose only by lowest price

Document scanning cost depends on:

- Page volume
- Paper condition
- Staples, bindings and preparation work
- OCR needs
- Manual indexing fields
- Onsite deployment
- Number of locations
- Timeline
- Output format

A cheap quote can become costly if files are poorly named, pages are missed, metadata is weak or the final archive is difficult to search.

For pricing factors, read the [document digitization cost guide](/posts/document-digitization-services-price-cost-india).

## Final checklist

Before approving a vendor, confirm:

- Clear scope and page estimate
- Scanning model: onsite or offsite
- Security process
- Batch tracking
- OCR and indexing fields
- Output format
- Quality-check process
- Timeline
- Case-study proof
- Final handover method
