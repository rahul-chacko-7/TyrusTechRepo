Many teams search for a records digitization company when they have old files, registers, cartons, paper records or department archives that need to be converted into digital form.

The phrase may be searched in different ways: records digitization company in India, record digitization services, old record digitalization, document digitization company, or records scanning company. The intent is usually the same: convert physical records into searchable digital files.

## What does records digitization mean?

Records digitization is the process of converting physical records into digital formats that can be stored, searched, shared and retrieved easily.

It can include:

- Physical file review
- Document preparation
- Scanning
- Image quality checks
- OCR text extraction
- Metadata capture
- Indexing
- File naming
- Folder structuring
- Secure digital handover

The final output may be searchable PDF, PDF/A, TIFF images, Excel or CSV index sheets, DMS-ready folders or cloud-ready archive files.

## What types of records can be digitized?

A records digitization company can handle many types of physical records, including:

- Old office files
- Government records and registers
- Medical records and MRD files
- KYC files and loan documents
- Legal case files and contracts
- HR employee folders
- Finance records, invoices and vouchers
- Student records and certificates
- Land records and citizen files
- Legacy cartons and inactive archive boxes

Some records are simple loose sheets. Others may be fragile, bound, stapled, folded, damaged or mixed with different page sizes. The scanning workflow should be planned based on the physical condition of the records.

## Why OCR and indexing matter

Scanning alone creates images. OCR and indexing make those images useful.

OCR helps convert scanned text into searchable text. Indexing captures important fields so users can find records quickly.

Common indexing fields include:

- File number
- Customer ID
- Patient ID or MRN
- Account number
- Branch
- Department
- Year
- Date
- Register number
- Invoice number
- Vendor name
- Record category

For old records or handwritten files, manual verification may be needed because OCR confidence can be lower.

## Onsite or offsite records digitization

Records can be digitized onsite or offsite.

Onsite digitization is useful when records are confidential, sensitive, restricted, active or difficult to move. A scanning team, equipment and supervisor can be deployed at the client location.

Offsite digitization may be practical when records can be packed, transported and tracked securely.

The right model depends on document sensitivity, location, page volume, timeline and available workspace.

## How to choose a records digitization company

Before choosing a provider, ask these questions:

- Can you handle old records, registers and mixed-size files?
- Do you provide bulk records scanning?
- Can you scan onsite if records cannot leave the premises?
- How do you track boxes, files, batches and missing pages?
- Do you provide OCR and searchable PDFs?
- Can you capture metadata fields?
- Can the output be DMS, EMR, ERP or cloud-ready?
- How do you handle damaged, unreadable or duplicate records?
- What quality checks are included?
- Can you share relevant case studies?
