Document digitization services in India price and cost depend on more than the number of pages. Page volume matters, but preparation, scanning location, OCR, indexing and delivery format can change the final estimate.

This guide explains the main cost factors so your team can budget realistically.

## 1. Page volume

Page volume is the biggest cost driver. A project with 5,000 pages and a project with 5 lakh pages need different manpower, scanner capacity, quality checks and delivery planning.

Higher volumes often get better per-page economics because setup costs are spread across more pages.

## 2. Document condition

Scanning clean A4 sheets is different from scanning:

- Stapled files
- Bound registers
- Torn or fragile pages
- Mixed-size documents
- Old carbon copies
- Faded or handwritten records

More preparation means more time before scanning starts.

## 3. Onsite vs offsite scanning

[Onsite document scanning](/onsite-document-scanning-services) may cost more than offsite scanning because scanners, operators, supervisors and workflows must be deployed at your location.

But onsite scanning is often the right choice for confidential records, active files, hospitals, banks, legal teams and government archives.

## 4. OCR and indexing

Basic scanning creates image files. OCR and indexing make files searchable and useful.

Cost changes based on:

- Searchable PDF requirement
- Number of metadata fields
- Manual verification needs
- DMS-ready output
- Excel or CSV index sheets
- Field accuracy expectations

For search-ready files, see [OCR indexing services](/ocr-indexing-services).

## 5. Output format

Common outputs include:

- PDF
- Searchable PDF
- PDF/A
- TIFF, JPEG or PNG
- Excel or CSV metadata
- DMS, EMR, ERP or cloud-ready folders

More structured output usually needs more planning and validation.

## 6. Timeline

Urgent projects may need more scanners, shifts, operators and supervisors. Flexible timelines are usually easier to optimize for cost.

## How to get a better quote

Share these details:

- Approximate page count
- Number of files, boxes or locations
- Document type
- Onsite or offsite preference
- OCR and indexing needs
- Output format
- Timeline
