Many people search for online document digitization services in India expecting a fully remote process. In practice, document digitization usually has two parts: physical paper handling and digital output delivery.

If your records are already scanned, the work can be mostly online. If your files are still paper, a scanning team must first convert them into digital files.

## What can be done online

Online digitization workflows can include:

- Uploading already-scanned PDFs or images
- OCR text extraction
- Metadata capture and indexing
- File naming and folder structuring
- Converting scanned files into searchable PDF
- Preparing DMS, EMR, ERP or cloud-ready output
- Secure digital handover

This is useful when your team already has scanned images but cannot search, organize or retrieve them properly.

## What needs physical scanning

If your documents are in cartons, files, registers, folders or record rooms, physical scanning is still required. Tyrus can plan [bulk document scanning services](/bulk-document-scanning-services) or [onsite document scanning services](/onsite-document-scanning-services) depending on security and logistics.

Onsite scanning is useful when records are confidential, active, legally restricted or difficult to move.

## Why OCR and indexing matter

Scanning creates a digital image. Digitization makes the file useful.

OCR and indexing can turn scanned images into:

- Searchable PDF files
- PDF/A archive files
- Excel or CSV metadata sheets
- Patient-wise, customer-wise or branch-wise folders
- DMS-ready digital archives

For this stage, see [OCR indexing services](/ocr-indexing-services).

## Who needs online digitization support

Online or hybrid digitization is common for:

- Hospitals with scanned medical records
- Banks with KYC and loan file images
- Legal teams with case bundles
- HR teams with employee file scans
- Government departments with legacy scan output
- Enterprises consolidating branch archives
