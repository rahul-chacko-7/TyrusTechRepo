Document digitization is not only for large enterprises with millions of pages. Small companies also need paper records converted into digital files, especially when documents are difficult to find, storage space is limited, or teams need easier access.

If your business has a small quantity of files, invoices, contracts, HR folders, customer records or office documents, a focused scanning project can still save time and reduce daily friction.

## When small quantity digitization makes sense

Small businesses often need digitization when:

- Office cupboards are full of old files
- Invoices and vouchers are needed for accounts or audit
- Contracts and agreements are stored physically
- HR documents need safer digital access
- Customer files are difficult to search
- Important records are at risk of damage or loss
- Teams want searchable PDF copies before moving to cloud storage

The project does not need to be huge. Even a few boxes or selected files can be scanned, indexed and organized.

## What can be digitized

Small quantity document scanning can include:

- Invoices and finance records
- Customer files
- Vendor documents
- HR and employee files
- Contracts and agreements
- Tax and compliance records
- Certificates and licenses
- Office administration files
- Old project documents

For many small companies, the goal is simple: find documents faster and reduce dependence on physical files.

## What output should you ask for

For a small digitization project, useful output includes:

- Searchable PDF files
- PDF/A archive copies
- Folder structure by year, department or document type
- Excel or CSV index sheet
- File names that match the document purpose
- Secure drive, cloud or DMS-ready handover

OCR is useful even for small projects because it lets users search inside scanned documents.

## Onsite or offsite scanning?

For small quantities, offsite scanning may be practical if documents can be safely moved. But onsite scanning may be better when files are confidential, active, sensitive or cannot leave the office.

Tyrus can suggest the right model after reviewing document type, quantity, location and security needs.

## How to prepare before requesting a quote

Before asking for a quote, prepare:

- Approximate number of files or pages
- Document types
- Number of boxes or folders
- Required output format
- Whether OCR is needed
- Whether documents can leave your office
- Timeline

This helps avoid confusion and gives a clearer estimate.

## Why small companies should not ignore indexing

Scanning creates digital images. Indexing makes them easy to find.

Even for small projects, decide basic search fields such as:

- Document type
- Year
- Customer name
- Invoice number
- Employee name
- Vendor name
- File number

Good naming and indexing will make the archive useful later.
